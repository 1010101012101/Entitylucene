What are the options for third party monitors with DVI or VGA?  <answer19099> They're the same as for other computers with Thunderbolt:  ul liDVI/VGA/HDMI with cheap passive Mini DisplayPort adapters (audio is included over HDMI, assuming your adapter supports it)/li lidual-link DVI with a more-expensive active Mini DisplayPort adapter/li liany native DisplayPort monitor (many mid-to-high-end monitors already have them)/li liany Thunderbolt monitor (all one of them currently announced)/li /ul  You can use any Mini DisplayPort adapter that you might already have, or any that are available for purchase. I have my brand-new 2011 MBA hooked up with a Monoprice MDP-DVI adapter right now.  <answer19100> The MB Air has a Thunderbolt port but you can also just plug in a Mini DisplayPort with an adapter to a pretty much any monitor or TV you want.
