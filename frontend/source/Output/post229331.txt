Can I put my Launch Daemon under code/Library/LaunchDaemons/code as a second one?  <answer229412> I haven't had to deal with this "for real" yet, but I think the best solution is to:  ol liCopy the .plist file into /Library/LaunchDaemons./li liRename it (e.g. by adding "local." to the beginning of the filename), and edit its codeLabel/code value to match. If you don't change this, launchd is likely to get confused between this and the original./li liMake whatever other edits you need/li liMake sure the ownership is set to root:wheel, and permissions to 644./li liDeactivate the original item with e.g. codesudo launchctl unload -w /System/Library/LaunchDaemons/org.postfix.master.plist/code (the code-w/code makes this permanent)./li liLoad your customized replacement with e.g. codesudo launchctl load /Library/LaunchDaemons/local.org.postfix.master.plist/code (or just reboot)./li /ol  BTW, if you do just disable SIP and modify the existing file, it won't necessarily be reverted by an OS update. But it emmight/em, and you'd have to check after every update (including security updates, etc), which is a pain.  <comment280455> Your solution works. Changing the `Label` value is indeed important.
