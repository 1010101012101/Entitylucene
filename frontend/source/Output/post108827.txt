CLI to copy to clipboard but still output to term ? <body> I know that piping to codepbcopy/code copies command line outputs to the clipboard.   How can I still show the output in the terminal ?   <answer108834> The tool of choice is called codetee/code. Full description is available via codeman tee/code but you canprobably just use  precodeecho test | tee &gt;(pbcopy) /code/pre  to get the result you are looking for.  <answer252088> You could just pbpaste immediately after.br codeecho test | pbcopy | pbpaste/code
