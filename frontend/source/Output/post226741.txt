MacBook Pro (13-inch Mid 2012), OS X El Capitan (10.11.3)  <answer231659> My MacBook (Late 2008 Unibody) does the same thing; once it's in the "deep deep sleep" state, it often fails to recognise that the lid has opened.  I've found that connecting and reconnecting the power adaptor with the lid open, then shut, then open again will make it "realise" that the the lid is open.  At this point, the keyboard becomes responsive (the caps lock LED will toggle if the key is pressed) and shortly afterwards the screen will light up.  A rather long-winded description of the sequence is:  ol liEnsure power adaptor is connected and MagSafe LED is illuminated./li liOpen lid./li liDisconnect power adaptor./li liReconnect power adaptor; wait for MagSafe LED to light and settle on a colour./li liClose lid./li liRepeat steps 3 and 4./li liRepeat steps 2 through 4./li /ol  My theory is that the Mac's System Management Controller (SMC) gets in a state where it's unaware that the lid has opened, and toggling the power adaptor in each state (lid open, lid closed) prompts it to re-check the lid status.  N.b This method emdoesn't/em work if the Mac is genuinely switched off, rather than sleeping - for example, if its battery completely discharged before it could hibernate.  <comment275357> P.S.
