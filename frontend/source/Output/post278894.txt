How do I take a Screenshot of the new "Touch Bar" on the newer Macs? <body> I am using Safari on the MacBook Pro, and had about 100 tabs open. As a result the Touch Bar lit up with lots of tiny images of the pages I opened.  I wanted to show someone how the Touch Bar had changed, but didn't want to take out my phone and take a picture of it.   Is there any way to do this?   <answer278897> Apple has released an update to the emGrab/em application that allows you to capture the Touch Bar.  img src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/AVreJ.jpg" alt="enter image description here"  The Touch Bar is constantly dynamic in nature, so Apple added a "timed" capture for the Touch Bar so you can navigate to the App with the Touch Bar layout before the "grab" executes.  You can find the emGrab/em application under Utilities.  kbd⇧ Shift/kbdkbd⌘ Command/kbdkbdU/kbd and select emGrab/em or use Spotlight (kbd⌘ Command/kbdkbdSpace/kbd) and just start typing "grab..."
