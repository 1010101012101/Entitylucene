What are you actually trying to do - learn how localized folder names work or how localization works in an app that's been internationalized with strings?  <answer266512> It's not as simple as changing the path based on the locale. The Photos library could be renamed and moved anywhere. You can find the current library by extracting the codeIPXDefaultLibraryURLBookmark/code from the Photos plist.  precode/usr/libexec/PlistBuddy -c "print IPXDefaultLibraryURLBookmark" \ ~/Library/Containers/com.apple.Photos/Data/Library/Preferences/com.apple.Photos.plist /code/pre  The IPXDefaultLibraryURLBookmark contains a CFURLRef. This will require deserialising.  <answer266513> The folder name is composed of code~/Pictures/${localpart}.photoslibrary//code, where code${localpart}/code stored in a code.strings/code file provided by the Photos app. To extract it, use this command:  precodeplutil -p /Applications/Photos.app/Contents/Resources/${lan}.lproj/IPXMain.strings |   grep "IPXDefaultLibraryName" |   cut -d"&gt;" -f2 | xargs /code/pre  where code${lan}/code is your operation system's language.
